II · The Art

Yangyiquan (阳意拳)

Etymology, Origin, and What This System Is

阳
First Character
Yáng · Light, Sun, Radiance

The luminous and active principle of the universe. In the system it symbolizes the internal illumination of consciousness and the radiant force of the warrior spirit.

意
Second Character
Yì · Intention, Mind, Will

The pulsing core of the system. The idea that precedes movement, the thought that governs Qi, the will that directs energy. Without Yi, there is no authentic Wushu.

拳
Third Character
Quán · Fist, Combat System

Not merely the physical strike — it designates the totality of a structured system of techniques, principles, and martial philosophy.

Complete Meaning
The Fist of the Light of Intention

Supremacy in combat resides not in muscular mass or raw speed, but in the clarity and purity of mental intention that precedes, accompanies, and transcends every physical action.

Yangyiquan was built upon the formal instruction I received from Shifu Diogo Sant'Ana in Yang Taijiquan, Chen Taijiquan, and Nanquan — and from Sensei Carmindo Paiva in Karate Shukokai. These foundations were transcended and forged into an autonomous martial language: a system complete in itself, philosophically coherent, technically functional, and culturally unique. It represents the first Chinese martial system developed from a Portuguese cultural perspective — the spirit of Portugal, land of fishermen and conquistadors — united with the Chinese martial heritage and the Islamic spiritual framework of its founder.

The system commands the complete fourfold taxonomy of Chinese martial arts: Tī (踢 — Kicks), Dǎ (打 — Strikes), Ná (拿 — Grabs and Joint Controls), and Shuāi (摔 — Throws and Takedowns). Its graduation is organized in 5 levels, from Genesis and Intention (Yì) at Level 1, through to Mastery, Vibration, and Unification at Level 5. At the pinnacle stands the Supreme Technique: Long Zhan Yi (龙展翼) — The Dragon Spreads His Wings.

III · Philosophy

The Three Supreme Virtues

Rěnnài · Shànyì · Kuānshù

Yangyiquan recognizes that external wars are always the reflection of unresolved internal wars. The entire practice rests upon three virtues which, when cultivated sincerely, prevent conflict, strengthen the spirit, and guarantee victory in every dimension of existence. These are not abstract ideals — they are functional martial tools.

I

Patience — Rěnnài (忍耐)

The Mastery of Suffering

In Yangyiquan, patience is not passive resignation — it is the active manifestation of inner mastery. The warrior who has cultivated patience has learned to absorb the blow of suffering as water absorbs a rock: without shattering, without retreating, maintaining essential nature intact. In combat, patience means waiting for the precise moment. The warrior who is impatient reveals his intentions, consumes energy in premature attacks, and collapses under fatigue. The patient warrior observes, accumulates information, concentrates force in the Dan Tian, and releases all his power at the exact instant when the opponent's opening is maximum.

II

Kindness — Shànyì (善意)

The Water That Gives Life

Kindness as a source of all durable force. Not naïve or sentimental kindness, but kindness as deep understanding of human nature — active compassion that perceives the adversary's suffering even in the moment of combat. The technique of Shíbié Bié Rén Xīn (识别别人心 — Identifying the Heart of Another) is its technical expression on the battlefield: the advanced practitioner develops the capacity to place himself in the adversary's emotional space, sensing their deepest fears and hidden intentions. This martial empathy does not weaken the warrior — it makes him infinitely more effective.

III

Forgiveness — Kuānshù (宽恕)

Karmic Liberation

Forgiveness is the most powerful weapon in the Yangyiquan arsenal. Not a sentimental concession toward those who have harmed us, but a strategic act of supreme spiritual intelligence: the liberation of karmic chains that bind us to the adversary and drain our vital energy. In Yangyiquan's language, not forgiving is equivalent to holding a closed fist around a ball of fire: the one who suffers most is the one holding the flame. Rancor, hatred, and the desire for revenge are poisons that contaminate the practitioner's Qi, block energy flow through the meridians, cloud the clarity of Yi, and ultimately irreversibly weaken martial capacities.

IV · Internal Work

The 9 Internal Powers

Nei Gong (內功) — The Foundation of the System's Superiority

Yangyiquan bases its technical and philosophical superiority upon a system of Nei Gong (內功 — Internal Work) of unprecedented depth in contemporary martial arts. This system, developed and articulated by Sultan Assad Abd-Al-Ghalib, consists of nine internal powers organized in two levels: The Four Extreme Powers (四极法 Sì Jí Fǎ) and The Five Intermediate Powers (五中法 Wǔ Zhōng Fǎ).

The Four Extreme Powers (四极法)

Extreme Power I
Yǐ Jīng (已经)
已经

"The Already Is" — Omniscient Vision, Immutable Reality, Supreme Mastery of Knowledge. The capacity to perceive reality in its totality as it already exists in complete and perfect form, though hidden from ordinary perception. The result of a mind purified through deep meditation, radical introspection, and emotional mastery.

Extreme Power II
Tiān (天)
天

Heaven — Spiritual and Celestial Forces, Prophecy, Harmony with Universal Laws. Connects the practitioner with universal laws governing all phenomena. When the warrior acts in perfect consonance with eternal principles of justice, truth, and compassion, Heaven's power flows through him, exponentially magnifying his effectiveness.

Extreme Power III
Dì Cí (地磁)
地磁

Earth-Magnet — Rootedness, Stability, Force Manifesting in the Human World. The gravitational and magnetic force of the Earth as a model of stable and lasting power. Teaches the practitioner to absorb Qi from the Earth through the feet, store it in the Dan Tian, and release it as controlled explosions of force — the basis of Fajin.

Extreme Power IV
Dì Yù (地狱)
地狱

The Underworld — Mastery of Hidden and Dark Forces, Transformation of Shadow Energies. The most feared and least understood power. The capacity to dominate, redirect, and dissolve energies that would otherwise undermine the warrior's inner balance. In combat: absorbing the aggressor's destructive energy, causing it to implode back upon them.

The Five Intermediate Powers (五中法)

Intermediate I
Shíbié Bié Rén Xīn
识别别人心

Identifying the Heart of Another — Martial Empathy, Deep Emotional Perception, Strategic Compassion. Placing oneself in the adversary's emotional space to sense motivations, fears, and hidden intentions. Also opens the door to Dào Zhìliáo — therapeutic healing through empathic resonance.

Intermediate II
Chuàng Zào (创造)
创造

Creation — Observing Nature to Create Techniques, Originality as Tactical Advantage. The warrior must observe animals, trees, rocks, wind — and with the hand, trace in the air the movement of their curves. This systematic protocol of applied biomechanical investigation generates techniques of surprising efficacy.

Intermediate III
Zhǎn (斩)
斩

The Cut — Cutting Unfavorable Links, Eliminating Fear, Spiritual Liberation. Both physical (the cutting blow, fingers as lance) and spiritual: deliberately cutting energetic, psychological, and karmic bonds that drain energy and diminish effectiveness in combat and life.

Intermediate IV
Cáng Bǎo (藏宝)
藏宝

The Hidden Treasure — Qi as Information, Breath as Weapon, Karma as Battlefield. Reveals the deeper nature of Qi: not merely "vital energy" but information — thoughts, intentions, and emotional states of all beings present in a combat field, floating in the air everyone breathes. "If you inhale in the face of fear, it no longer dominates you."

Intermediate V
Zhì (制)
制

Mastery — Control of Yin and Yang, The Work of the Immortal, Cosmic Equilibrium. Complete and conscious mastery of the polar forces of the universe in one's own being and environment. "He who holds all Yin and Yang within himself becomes a universe." The supreme goal of Nei Gong.

V · Technical Arsenal

Signature Techniques

Shu Fa (术法) · Shen Fa (身法) — Methods of the Body

All techniques of Yangyiquan share fundamental biomechanical principles: the Relaxation-Explosion Principle (muscles remain completely relaxed during the technique's trajectory and contract explosively only at the millisecond of impact); the Ascending Kinetic Chain Principle (force originates in the feet-earth connection and rises through knees, hips, spine, shoulder to the point of impact); Double Hand-Action Principle (the non-attacking hand is always active); and the Principle of Attack on Sensitive Points (eyes, throat, solar plexus, genitals, knees, neck).

Long Di Quan
龙地拳
Dragon Fist of Earth

Devastating horizontal strike executed from guard position with complete hip rotation. The fist travels parallel to the ground — symbolizing terrestrial stability — generating force through the kinetic chain feet-knees-hips-spine-shoulder-elbow-fist. The rear hand remains retracted at the hip, simulating a prior grab that amplifies power through counter-tension. Final impact produced with Fajin (发劲) — explosive emission of energy from the Dan Tian. Applications: face, solar plexus, sternum, genitals, neck, floating ribs.

Long Shan Zhao
龙山爪
Dragon Extends the Claw

Penetrating attack with fingers in claw or lance formation, aimed at soft zones of maximum neurological vulnerability. Two-phase technique: first phase of distraction/control (front hand creates visual threat), second phase of definitive strike (rear hand executes real attack exploiting the opening created). The finger extension generates an energetic continuation beyond the fingertips that penetrates the adversary's energetic field before physical contact. Applications: eyes (tactical blindness), throat, solar plexus, pressure points.

Long Tian Quan
龙天拳
Dragon of Heaven Fist

Ascending vertical strike symbolizing the line connecting Earth to Heaven. Maximum power generated through complete Fajin from the feet: the wave of force is born in the Earth (Dì Cí), rises through feet, knees, and hips, amplifies in the Dan Tian, and is released through the fist at the moment of maximum muscular tension. The Shang Dan Tian (Third Eye) is invoked for maximum spiritual force. Applications: chin and jaw (knockout by mandibular nerve compression), solar plexus, genitals, sternum.

Long Wei Bian
龙尾鞭
Dragon Tail Whip

Dual technique of block and counterattack imitating the movement of a dragon's tail: rises to block and discharges downward to attack. The ascending phase intercepts any incoming attack. The descending phase — the real whip — generates devastating impact through the centrifugal acceleration of the arm from the shoulder. Applications: neck, clavicle, cervical spine, shoulder. Can chain into finger penetration to throat or eyes, or headbutt.

Long Zhan Yi
龙展翼
Dragon Opens Its Wings — Supreme Technique

Explosive thoracic expansion with both arms opening simultaneously toward the sides in an ascending circular trajectory, synchronized with a deep and powerful Dan Tian inhalation. The opening generates a spherical field of protection around the body, knocking down or redirecting any grab or clinch attempt. The subsequent closing produces impact: palms strike both ears simultaneously (tympanic rupture, severe disorientation), forearms strike ribs bilaterally. The Kiai — vocal force — may be added at the moment of opening to amplify the psychological paralyzing effect.
